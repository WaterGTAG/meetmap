Meetmap Terms of Use

Updated August 9, 2025
Meetmap is an app that allows people to easily manage their status (a set of buttons used to represent your real-life status for a specific event) and view others’ statuses within a meetup (a page for events where people can view everyone’s statuses and change their own).
We try our best to make a fun and easy-to-use app for meetups—big or small—to manage their attendees.
By creating a Meetmap account, you agree to these terms. Any violation of these terms may result in account termination.

Account Requirements

To comply with the U.S. Children’s Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), all registered users must be 13 years of age or older.
Users must ensure their account content is appropriate. Names, usernames, or profile pictures containing sexually explicit, discriminatory, or graphic content are prohibited.

Meetup Requirements

Meetups must be appropriate for all 13+ users. Just like account rules, any meetup names or icons containing sexually explicit, discriminatory, or graphic content are prohibited.
As there are currently no filters or modes for events promoting the use of age-restricted items (e.g., alcohol, gambling, drugs, smoking, etc.), events that encourage or advertise the use of such items are not allowed.
This only applies if the actual event itself is related to or promotes age-restricted items. If a meetup takes place at a venue that offers age-restricted items but the event is not directly related to them, you may still use Meetmap—but it is your responsibility (or the venue’s responsibility) to ensure no illegal activity occurs.
Please ensure that all meetups on Meetmap follow local laws.
